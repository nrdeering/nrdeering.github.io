Citi Bike Ride Analysis

Use data collected by Citi Bike
from the period of January to June 2019
and create a Tableau dashboard to analyze
and answer questions on the data.

Get Started

Objective

Aggregate the data found in the Citi Bike Trip History Logs to build a data dashboard answering key questions that city officials are looking to answer:

How many trips have been recorded total during the chosen period?
By what percentage has total ridership grown?
What are the peak hours in which bikes are used during summer months?
What are the peak hours in which bikes are used during winter months?
Today, what are the top 10 stations in the city for starting a journey?
Today, what are the top 10 stations in the city for ending a journey?
Today, what are the bottom 10 stations in the city for starting a journey?
Today, what are the bottom 10 stations in the city for ending a journey?
Today, what is the gender breakdown of active participants (Male v. Female)?
How does the average trip duration change by age?
What is the average distance in miles that a bike is ridden?
A static map that plots all bike stations with a visual indication of the most popular locations to start and end a journey with zip code data overlaid on top.

The audience (hypothetically) are city officials, public administrators, and heads of New York City departments. The data and analysis needs to be presented in a way that is focused, concise, easy-to-understand, and visually compelling. The visualizations should be colorful enough to be included in press releases, and the analysis should be thoughtful enough for dictating programmatic changes.

Conclusion

Looking at the dashboard, let's answer a few of the questions asked by city officials; From January to June, ridership increased 115.4%. Looking at the peak hours per month, the graphs show that the peak hours are roughly 8am and 5pm, usually the times most people travel to and from work. In the same graph, you can see the peaks grow taller as the summer months approach. With these two graphs, we can assume that there are more rides in the warmer summer months than the winter months. The dashboard gives us a look into the average ride duration by age, and to my surprise, folks born in 1967-1976 had the highest average trip duration, followed closely by the age group (which I had assumed would be the group with the highest average trip before making the visual) born from 1997-2003. Finally, the dotted heat maps, along with the top and bottom 10 starting/ending stations give us a look into the city's hotspots and deadzones and gives an idea on how to better utilize the bike inventory across the city.
